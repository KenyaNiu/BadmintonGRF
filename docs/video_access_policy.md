## BadmintonGRF Video Access Policy (Application-Based)

BadmintonGRF includes raw multi-view videos of human participants. To protect privacy and comply with consent and institutional requirements, **raw videos are not released publicly**. Instead, video access is provided via an application-based process for qualified research use.

### What is publicly released
We release the **processed benchmark data** (e.g., 2D keypoints, GRF, synchronization metadata, impact segments, LOSO splits, and documentation), enabling most machine learning and biomechanics research without requiring raw videos.

### What is available by application
Applicants may request access to the **raw multi-view videos** for approved research purposes.

### Eligibility
We typically approve requests from:
- Academic or non-profit research groups
- Researchers with an IRB/ethics approval or equivalent institutional clearance (if required by your institution)
- Projects aligned with biomechanics, sports analytics, human motion analysis, or related research

### Key terms (summary)
By requesting access, applicants agree that they will:
- Use the videos **only for non-commercial research and education**
- **Not attempt to identify** participants (no re-identification)
- **Not use** the videos for face recognition, surveillance, biometric profiling, or any purpose that increases privacy risk
- **Not redistribute** the videos (including re-uploading to public clouds, sharing with third parties, or releasing derivatives that enable reconstruction)
- Store the videos securely and limit access to authorized team members
- Cite BadmintonGRF in publications that use the videos or derived results

### Derivatives
Publishing results (metrics, plots, model weights) is permitted. Publishing derived data should be done with care:
- Sharing **2D keypoints and anonymized statistics** is generally acceptable.
- Sharing any derivative that can reconstruct identifiable video content is not permitted.

### How to apply
Submit the request form:
- `docs/video_access_request_form.md`

We may ask for:
- Project title and short description
- Institution and lab/PI
- Intended use and whether an IRB/ethics approval exists
- Data storage plan (where it will be stored, who can access it)

### Review timeline
We aim to respond within **7–14 days** (may vary).
